Welcome to Airline Manager 4

This guide is designed for new players who want to grow fast and efficiently. Follow these steps to build a strong fleet and manage your airline like a pro.

🛫 Getting Started

  • Start with the DC-9-10 – second best starter plane.
  • Next step: buy B727-100s.
  • Once you have a fleet of 10 x B727-100, you're making decent money.

📈 Fleet Expansion

  • Upgrade to MC-21-400 or B737-800.
  • Add many of them – anywhere from 30 to 150 units.
  • Then step up to A340-200 or B787-8 for mid-priced long-range routes.
Don't touch wages yet. Just keep expanding your fleet. Within 10–12 days, you'll earn enough training points to unlock the first page of Management Training.

💡 The Million Dollar Tip

  1. Complete Management Training.
  2. Issue pay cuts until you see: “Salary cannot be lowered if morale is below 50%”.
  3. Raise salary until morale reaches 100%.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 until you reach minimum salary.

Watch the tutorial on YouTube: Search “Million Dollar Tip AM4”.

🏢 Hubs & Reputation

Owning 10 hubs gives you a permanent +9% reputation boost (from 45% to 54%) → more passengers per flight.

📣 Marketing & Eco

Always activate Marketing and Eco before launching your fleet in the morning – more passengers = more profit.

🏦 Avoid the Bank

Don't use the bank – no interest and they charge for withdrawals. Instead, invest in good aircraft, hubs, and hangars.

🌍 Easy Mode Complex Guide

1️⃣ The First Steps (Day 1–3)

  • Pick a large international hub (LHR, JFK, DXB, FRA, SIN, HND).
  • Lease B737-800 or A321-XLR (3–5 units).
  • Use short/medium haul routes (2–6 hrs).
  • 100% Economy config. Auto-price → raise if occupancy >95%.

2️⃣ Expansion Phase (Day 4–14)

  • Fleet: 10–20 planes. Keep leasing.
  • Unlock 2nd hub when first reaches ~90% capacity.
  • Use route search tool. Avoid oversupply.
  • R&D: Maintenance, Marketing, Service Quality.

3️⃣ Scaling Up (Weeks 3–6)

  • Add A350-900/1000 or B787-9/10 for long-haul.
  • Mix: 50–60% narrowbodies, 40–50% widebodies.
  • Build lounges in hubs with 10+ long-haul planes.
  • Switch to 60% Business / 40% First Class configs.

4️⃣ Advanced Growth (Months 2–6)

  • Expand to 5–10 hubs. 1 hub per 20 planes.
  • Use A380-800 only if demand is very high.
  • Fill major intercontinental routes first.
  • Marketing in hubs with 80%+ route coverage.

5️⃣ Long-Term Domination (6+ Months)

  • Research Completion: Finish all R&D (except fuel and CO₂ in Easy Mode).
  • Marketing & Service: Max out service quality and marketing to attract loyal passenger flow.
  • Automated Scheduling: Once you reach 100+ planes, use scheduling tools. Group aircraft by type for efficiency.
  • VIP Routes Strategy: In your top 5 hubs, assign 3 widebodies per hub with premium-heavy layouts (60% Business, 40% First) on high-demand long-haul routes.
  • Finance Optimization: Once you're wealthy, reduce leasing. Buying is cheaper long-term. Keep a cash reserve but reinvest aggressively.
  • Event Participation: Join global events (cargo, holidays, etc.) — they offer huge profit multipliers.
⚡ Golden Rules for Easy Mode:
• Expand fast — no fuel/CO₂ constraints.
• Prioritize big hubs — higher demand = higher profits.
• Balance your fleet — narrowbody feeders + widebody profit-makers.
• Use lounges + premium seats — the ultimate money-printing combo.
• Reinvest constantly — cash should always be working, not sitting idle.

🧠 Expert Tips & Final Advice

  • Daily Routine: Log in every morning, activate Marketing & Eco, check route occupancy, and adjust ticket prices.
  • Fleet Grouping: Group aircraft by type and range (short-haul, medium-haul, long-haul) for easier scheduling and upgrades.
  • Hangar Strategy: Build hangars in hubs with high aircraft density to reduce maintenance costs and downtime.
  • Time Zones: Launch flights based on local time zones of departure hubs to maximize passenger flow.
  • Seasonal Routes: Add seasonal destinations (e.g., ski resorts, beach cities) during peak months for bonus profits.
  • Fleet Retirement: Sell or retire older aircraft once maintenance costs outweigh profits. Replace with newer models.
  • Competitor Watch: Monitor other airlines’ hubs and routes. Avoid oversaturation and find underserved markets.
✨ Final Thought: Easy Mode is your playground — experiment boldly, expand aggressively, and optimize smartly. The sky isn’t the limit. It’s just the beginning.
