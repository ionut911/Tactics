‚úàÔ∏è 3. Starter Aircraft & First Route Strategy

At the beginning of the game, you'll be given a starter aircraft. Either option works, but the 737-800 is generally preferred for its range, speed, and earning potential.

Choosing Your First Route

  • If the game suggests a long route, cancel and retry until you get a short one.
  • Wait for your starter plane to return to your hub before activating the 4x speedup bonus.
  • Short routes earn more money as long as you're actively dispatching flights on arrival.
  • Example: Orlando to Sarasota Bradenton is short enough; Orlando to Melbourne, FL is too short.

Why Short Routes Matter

Short routes allow for frequent departures, which means more revenue per hour ‚Äî especially during the 4x speedup window. The key is to keep your aircraft moving constantly.

üì¢ 5. Marketing Campaigns

Marketing can be powerful ‚Äî but timing matters. Early in the game, regular marketing is not a great return on investment unless you're departing more than 250 empty seats per hour.

  • They help fill seats and boost reputation at a low cost.
  • Only skip eco-marketing if you're flying fewer than 200 empty seats in the next 12 hours.

  • Marketing costs scale slowly as your airline grows ‚Äî so early investment pays off.

As your airline expands, regular marketing becomes more viable. If you're growing fast, you'll likely benefit from full campaigns within a couple of days.

üèÜ 7. Achievements & Speedup Bonus

One of the key early-game prizes is the 4-hour 4x speedup

Easy Achievements to Target

  • Advertiser: Launch a top-tier 4-hour marketing campaign
  • Aircraft Expert: Buy any aircraft
  • Airline Owner: Automatically granted when you start the game
  • Capital Operator: Run a route to a major airport or capital city
  • Ferry: Launch a flight with no passengers (use the Cessna)
  • Recall: Recall a launched flight (Cessna is cheapest)
  • Map Operator: Change the map stack and enable dark mode
  • Stopping Up: Add a stopover when setting a route (no need to fly it)

Claiming the Speedup

Once your starter aircraft has landed and you've earned 5 achievements, go to the checklist and claim your 4-hour 4x speedup. Activate it only when you're ready to commit to focused gameplay ‚Äî this is your most productive window.

Optimizing the Speedup Window

  • Depart aircraft on short, single-hop routes
  • Avoid changing routes during speedup ‚Äî reconfiguring costs money
  • Assign only one aircraft per destination to avoid saturating demand
  • Order more aircraft as soon as you can afford them

‚úàÔ∏è 11. Favorite Mid-Game Aircraft

As you move beyond the starter fleet and early-game grind, certain aircraft stand out for their efficiency, range, and upgrade potential. These picks help bridge the gap between short-haul spam and long-haul dominance.

Top Mid-Game Aircraft Picks

  • ERJ 145LR: Cheap but not too cheap. Fast and long-range for its price bracket. Can handle up to ~4-hour routes with stopovers. Efficient enough to avoid losses in most scenarios.
  • Tu 334-220: Slightly larger and more efficient. Also ~4-hour range with stopovers. Short runway capable, giving access to more route options. Great stepping stone before 737-800s.
  • 737-800: Versatile and powerful. Can run 6+ hour legs in easy mode. Purchased models fly slightly slower than the starter one, but both can be upgraded for +10% speed.
  • B787-8: Excellent range and fuel efficiency. Can run 11‚Äì12 hour hops, allowing for 2 departures per day instead of 4. Keeps flying while short-range planes sit idle.

Stopovers vs Direct Routes

  • Stopovers offer strategic flexibility but require more planning and micromanagement.
  • Direct routes are easier to research, configure, and maintain ‚Äî especially with a growing fleet.
  • Many players enjoy stopovers early on, but eventually shift to direct routes for simplicity.

Upgrade Strategy

  • Move up to the next aircraft tier when you can afford one in less than a day.
  • Sell smaller planes once your fleet approaches max capacity and you‚Äôre ready for long-haul efficiency.
